## Step 1 — Start with Conditional Probability

The definition of conditional probability is:

$$
P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}
$$

Meaning:

> Probability that **A happens given B already happened**

---

Similarly,

$$
P(B|A) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}
$$

Meaning:

> Probability that **B happens given A happened**

---

## Step 2 — Notice the common term

Both formulas contain the same joint probability:

$$
P(A \cap B)
$$

So we can write:

$$
P(A \cap B) = P(A|B)P(B)
$$

and also

$$
P(A \cap B) = P(B|A)P(A)
$$

Both represent the **same probability**.

---

## Step 3 — Equate them

Since both equal $P(A \cap B)$:

$$
P(A|B)P(B) = P(B|A)P(A)
$$

Now divide by $P(B)$:

$$
P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}
$$

That is **Bayes theorem**.

---

## Simple Example 

Suppose:

* 1% of people have a disease
* Test catches disease **99% of the time**

But 5% of healthy people also test positive.

Here:

* $P(\text{Positive}|\text{Disease}) = 0.99$

But

* $P(\text{Disease}|\text{Positive})$ is **much smaller**

Because most positives come from healthy people.

So:

**P(A|B) and P(B|A) can be very different.**
