
## 1. Start from the decision boundary

Like in Logistic Regression, we first assume a linear separator.

$$
w^T x + c = 0
$$

This is the **decision boundary**.

---

## 2. Why (w) is perpendicular to the boundary

The boundary is defined by all points satisfying:

$$
w^T x + c = 0
$$

Ignore (c) temporarily to understand geometry.

$$
w^T x = 0
$$

Using dot product definition:

$$
w^T x = |w||x|\cos\theta
$$

If this equals zero:

$$
|w||x|\cos\theta = 0
$$

Since magnitudes are non-zero, this means

$$
\cos\theta = 0
$$

so

$$
\theta = 90^\circ
$$

Meaning **w is perpendicular to vectors lying along the boundary**.

So:

**w is the normal vector of the decision boundary.**

This is the key geometric property used in Support Vector Machine.

---

## 3. Sign tells which side of the boundary

For any point (x):

$$
w^T x + c
$$

* positive → point lies **above the boundary**
* negative → point lies **below the boundary**

Because the sign depends on the **angle with (w)**.

---

## 4. Define the margin planes

SVM introduces two parallel planes:

Upper margin:

$$
w^T x + c = 1
$$

Lower margin:

$$
w^T x + c = -1
$$

They are **parallel** because the normal vector (w) is the same.

---

## 5. Compute the margin width

Take points $x_1$ and $x_2$ lying on these planes.

Upper plane:

$$
w^T$x_1$+ c =$1
$$
Lower plane:

$$
w^T x_2 + c = -1
$$

Subtract:

$$
w^T(x_1 - x_2) = 2
$$

Now divide by the magnitude of (w).

The distance between planes becomes

$$
\text{margin} = \frac{2}{||w||}
$$

---

## 6. Optimization objective

We want the **largest margin**.

Maximize:

$$
\frac{2}{||w||}
$$

This is equivalent to minimizing

$$
||w||
$$

Usually written as

$$
\frac{1}{2}||w||^2
$$

because it simplifies optimization.

---

## 7. Hard margin constraint

For correctly classified points:

$$
y_i (w^T x_i + c) \ge 1
$$

Where

$$
y_i \in {-1, +1}
$$

Meaning:

* positive class → above upper margin
* negative class → below lower margin

---

## 8. Soft margin (real world data)

Real data is messy.

Some points fall inside the margin.

We introduce slack variables $ \xi_i $.

Objective becomes

$$
\min \frac{1}{2}||w||^2 + C \sum_{i=1}^{n} \xi_i
$$

Where:

* $C$ controls penalty for violations
* $\xi_i$ measures margin violation

This leads to the **hinge loss formulation**.

---

# Final intuition

SVM is basically solving:

**Find the hyperplane that separates classes with the largest margin.**

It does that by:

* minimizing $||w||$
* penalizing margin violations
