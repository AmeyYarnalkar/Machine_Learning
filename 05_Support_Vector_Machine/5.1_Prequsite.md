### when you already have

You wrote:

$$
w_1 x_1 + w_2 x_2 + b = 0
$$

This is just the equation of a line (decision boundary in 2D).

---

###  Now look at this carefully

We define:

* Weight vector → $ w = [w_1, w_2] $
* Input vector → $ x = [x_1, x_2] $

Now I’ll ask you something 👇

👉 What is the dot product of these two vectors?

Try to recall:
$$
w \cdot x = ?
$$

---

### 🔥 Key idea

The dot product is:

$$
w \cdot x = w_1 x_1 + w_2 x_2
$$

So your original equation becomes:

$$
w \cdot x + b = 0
$$

---

###  Final notation

Instead of writing dot as “·”, we write:

$$
w^T x + b = 0
$$

Why?

* $ w^T $ = transpose of vector $ w $
* Makes matrix multiplication valid
