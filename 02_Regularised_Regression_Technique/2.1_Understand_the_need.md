# Regularisation deals with overfitting.

## What is overfitting 

👉 Overfitting =
**Model memorizes the data instead of learning the pattern**

---

### Real-life analogy (don’t skip this)

Imagine:

You have past exam papers.

### Student A:

* Understands concepts
* Can solve new questions

### Student B:

* Memorizes all past answers
* Fails when questions change

👉 Student B = **overfitting model**

---

### Origin of overfitting (where it starts)

It starts when:

👉 **Model becomes too flexible compared to the data**

Meaning:

* Too many features
* Too complex model
* Too much freedom to bend

---

### Exact example (polynomial)

You used:

* degree = 2 → good (captures curve)

But if you used:

* degree = 10

👉 Model would:

* Twist and turn
* Pass through every point perfectly

But…

👉 It’s not learning reality \
👉 It’s memorizing noise

---

### What actually causes overfitting


---

### 1. Too complex model

* High polynomial degree
* Deep decision trees
* Too many parameters

👉 Model becomes “too smart” → memorizes noise

---

### 2. Small dataset

* You have ~100 points

👉 Not bad, but still:

* Easier to memorize than generalize

---

### 3. Noisy data

* Real-world data has randomness

👉 Model tries to fit noise → bad

---

### 4. Too many features

* More features than needed

👉 Model finds fake relationships

---

### 5. Training too hard (in some models)

* Like neural networks trained too long

---

###  What’s actually happening inside

Model tries to minimize error:

👉 It doesn’t know:

* what is signal
* what is noise

So it says:

> “Let me fit EVERYTHING perfectly”

That’s where it goes wrong.

---

## How to KNOW overfitting is happening

###  1. Train vs Test gap (biggest signal)

👉 If:

* Train accuracy = very high
* Test accuracy = much lower

Example:

```text id="1f7v9c"
Train = 99%
Test = 75%
```

👉 That’s overfitting. No debate.

---

### 2. Model performs badly on new data

* Works great on known data
* Fails on unseen data

---

### 3. Residuals behave weirdly

* On training → very small errors
* On test → large errors

---

### 4. Model too sensitive

Slight change in data → big change in prediction
