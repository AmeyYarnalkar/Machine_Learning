## DBSCAN — Brief Notes

### What is DBSCAN

**DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise)** is a clustering algorithm that groups **dense regions of data points** and labels sparse points as **noise (anomalies/outliers)**.

---

### Main Idea

Clusters are areas where **many points are close together**.

Points that **do not belong to any dense region** are treated as **outliers**.

---

### Two Important Parameters

**1. ε (epsilon)**
Maximum distance between two points for them to be considered neighbors.

**2. MinPts**
Minimum number of points required to form a dense region.

---

### Types of Points

**Core Point**
A point with **at least MinPts neighbors within ε distance**.

**Border Point**
A point that is **near a core point but has fewer than MinPts neighbors**.

**Noise Point (Outlier)**
A point that **is not reachable from any core point**.

---

### Basic Algorithm Steps

1. Pick a point.
2. Check how many neighbors are within **ε distance**.
3. If neighbors ≥ **MinPts**, it becomes a **core point** and a cluster starts.
4. Expand the cluster by including all reachable points.
5. Points not belonging to any cluster become **noise (outliers)**.

---

### Key Properties

• Detects **clusters of arbitrary shapes**
• Automatically identifies **outliers**
• No need to specify **number of clusters (k)**

---

### Advantages

* Works well with **irregular cluster shapes**
* Good for **anomaly detection**
* No need to predefine number of clusters

---

### Limitations

* Choosing **ε and MinPts** is tricky
* Struggles with **varying density datasets**
* Performance decreases with **high-dimensional data**

---

### One-Line Intuition

> **DBSCAN forms clusters where points are dense and marks isolated points as anomalies.**
