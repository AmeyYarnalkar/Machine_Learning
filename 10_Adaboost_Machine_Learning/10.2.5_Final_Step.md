
## Final Step 
##  Combine All Weak Learners

After repeating the boosting process for **M weak learners (decision stumps)**, AdaBoost builds the **final model** by combining all weak learners using their **model weights (α)**.

Each weak learner contributes to the final prediction **according to its performance**.

Better learners → **higher weight**
Weaker learners → **lower weight**

---

## Final Model Representation

AdaBoost

The final model is a **weighted sum of all weak learners**:

$$
F(x) = \sum_{m=1}^{M} \alpha_m h_m(x)
$$

Where:

* $M$$ = number of weak learners
* $h_m(x))$$ = prediction of the (m^{th}) weak learner
* $\alpha_m$$ = weight (performance) of that learner

---

## Final Prediction — Classification

In classification, each weak learner predicts a **class label**.

The final decision is obtained using **weighted voting**.

Each model votes with strength **α**.

The class receiving the **highest weighted vote** becomes the final prediction.

Mathematically:

$$
\text{Final Class} =
\text{sign}\left(\sum_{m=1}^{M} \alpha_m h_m(x)\right)
$$

So the algorithm essentially chooses the class with the **highest weighted support from all weak learners**.

---

# Final Prediction — Regression

Here is the correction.

For regression we **do not pick the model with the highest α**.

Instead we compute a **weighted average of predictions**.

$$
F(x) = \frac{\sum_{m=1}^{M} \alpha_m h_m(x)}
{\sum_{m=1}^{M} \alpha_m}
$$

So the final prediction is the **weighted average of all weak learner outputs**.

Each learner contributes according to its **performance weight**.

---


## Full AdaBoost Workflow (Clean Summary)

1. Initialize sample weights
2. Train the first **decision stump**
3. Compute **weighted error**
4. Compute **model weight (α)**
5. Update sample weights
6. Repeat steps for **M weak learners**
7. Combine all models using **weighted voting / averaging**

---

# One Important Practical Insight

AdaBoost works best when the weak learner is a **decision stump**.

Because if the trees become **too deep**, boosting may **overfit**.

That’s why most implementations use:

```text
max_depth = 1
```
